Section 01

The Idea

Why does Lab Zero need to exist at CGIT?

Right now, a CS student with an idea for a robot has nowhere to build it. A Mechanical student who wants to write code has no machine to try it on. An ECE student ready to present a project has no proper setup to show it.

Every department works in its own bubble. Students have drive and curiosity — but not the shared space, tools, or community of peers from other fields who could push them further.

Lab Zero changes that. It is the first open, cross-department innovation space at CGIT — purpose-built for collaboration, experimentation, and building things that matter.

As the founding batch of CS AI&ML and Robotics & AI at CGIT, we hold a meaningful opportunity and responsibility to create something that every batch after us can grow within. Lab Zero is that foundation.

Lab Zero is not just a room with equipment. It is the first open, cross-department innovation space at CGIT — where a Mechanical student, a CS student, and an ECE student can sit at the same table and build something none of them could alone.

Problem

Departments work in isolation. Cross-discipline projects rarely happen due to lack of space.

Solution

One shared, supervised space with the tools and culture to make collaboration the default.

Section 02

Five Innovation Zones

One shared open room — five areas of focused activity. Students naturally cross over, collaborate, and learn from each other.

A
Digital & Computing
  • High-performance workstations
  • Raspberry Pi & Arduino kits
  • Open-source dev environment
  • AI/ML project compute
B
Electronics & Embedded
  • Soldering stations
  • ESP32, STM32, Arduino stock
  • Oscilloscopes & multimeters
  • Sensors & component library
C
Mechanical & Making
  • 3D printer
  • Power & hand tools
  • Drill press
  • Prototyping materials
D
Presentation & Ideas
  • Smart projector
  • Speaker setup
  • Whiteboard walls
  • Flexible open seating
E
Server & Infrastructure Learning
  • Raspberry Pi cluster
  • Local project storage (NAS)
  • Practice networking node
  • Controlled edu. internet access

Zone E operates on a dedicated VLAN configured and approved by the college IT administration. All student activities within this zone are isolated from the main campus network and conducted under faculty supervision in accordance with college IT policies.

Section 05

Governance, Safety & Compliance

Lab Zero is designed to meet institutional, safety, and regulatory standards from day one — fully aligned with CGIT's administrative framework.

👨‍🏫

Faculty Supervision Structure

A designated Faculty Advisor oversees all Lab Zero operations with sign-off authority on equipment use, tier advancement, and project activities. Regular reviews with HOD.

📋

Equipment Usage Policy

All equipment use is logged in a physical register. Tier 2+ members may use equipment only after completing safety training and signing the usage policy document.

🦺

Safety Training Requirements

Mandatory safety orientation before any hands-on activity. Specific training required for tools, soldering equipment, and electrical components. Faculty-conducted annually.

🔐

Access Control Procedure

Physical access only during designated lab hours. All entries logged. Faculty advisor must be reachable during all active sessions. After-hours access requires written approval.

📦

Asset Register Maintenance

A formal asset register of all equipment, components, and donated materials is maintained, updated quarterly, and submitted to the HOD and accounts department.

🌐

IT Policy Compliance

Zone E's dedicated VLAN is configured and maintained by the college IT administration. Student activities operate within isolated environments per college IT policy. No modification of main campus network.

📜

Code of Conduct

All members sign a code of conduct covering responsible equipment use, academic integrity, respectful collaboration, and consequences for violations. Enforced by the Faculty Advisor.

🚨

Incident Reporting Mechanism

Any equipment damage, safety concern, or policy breach is documented and reported to the Faculty Advisor and HOD within 24 hours via a standardized incident report form.

🔄

Annual Review Process

Lab Zero's constitution, membership records, project outcomes, asset register, and compliance status are reviewed annually by the Faculty Advisor and HOD with a written report to IQAC.

Section 08

Roadmap

A phased plan — from permission to a permanently recognized institutional facility.

Week 1–2

Phase 1 — Get Permission

Submit proposal to HOD and Principal. Identify one faculty advisor. Identify a suitable unused room. Form a 5-member founding committee across departments. No budget required.

Month 1

Phase 2 — Soft Launch

Set up with existing college equipment and founding members' own gear. Run the first open session. Begin documentation. Create the Lab Zero identity. First faculty advisor meeting.

Month 2–3

Phase 3 — First Projects

First cross-department projects begin. Apply for e-Yantra kit. Request IT department approval for dedicated VLAN. Run first inter-department workshop. Document all outcomes for IQAC.

Semester 2

Phase 4 — Apply for Funding

With a working lab and documented outcomes, explore AICTE IDEA Lab application. Initiate NMDC CSR outreach. Register SIH team. Outcomes report submitted to HOD and IQAC.

Year 2

Phase 5 — Official Recognition

Lab Zero becomes a formally recognized institutional facility with budget, constitution, and annual intake. Founding batch transitions to mentor roles. Handover documentation complete.

Section 09

Our Request

The institution's contribution is minimal. The potential impact is substantial. We are seeking four specific approvals.

01

Official Permission

Permission to establish Lab Zero as an officially recognized faculty-supervised, student-led body at CGIT Jagdalpur, operating under the academic purview of the HOD and Principal.

02

A Room

Allocation of one unused room or existing underutilized lab space for Lab Zero operations. No structural modifications are required at this stage.

03

Faculty Advisor

Appointment of one faculty member as Lab Advisor — a supervisory and sign-off role. Active involvement is welcomed but not required beyond oversight and periodic review.

04

IT Department Approval — VLAN

Permission for the IT department to configure a dedicated student VLAN on the existing campus network. All activity within this segment is isolated from the main college network and governed by IT policy.

The institution is invited to provide a structured, supervised space for innovation. Lab Zero's founding team will handle the building — the college simply opens the door. This initiative represents a meaningful step in advancing CGIT's academic mission and the professional development of its students.
