# LAB ZERO — Proposal

## Overview

**LAB ZERO** is a faculty-supervised, student-led innovation space at CGIT Jagdalpur — the first open, cross-department collaborative workspace designed to break departmental silos and foster hands-on, outcome-based learning.

Proposed by the **CS AI&ML & Robotics AI** batches (2025–2029), Lab Zero envisions a shared space where students from Computer Science, Electronics, Mechanical Engineering, and other disciplines come together to design, build, and iterate on real projects.

## The Core Idea

Right now:
- A CS student with a robotics idea has no fabrication space
- An Mechanical student who wants to code has no computing resources
- Cross-department collaboration is rare due to lack of shared infrastructure

**Lab Zero changes that.** It's not just equipment — it's a culture of innovation, collaboration, and outcome-driven learning that prepares students for industry realities.

## Five Innovation Zones

Lab Zero is organized into five specialized zones within one open space:

| Zone | Focus | Equipment |
|------|-------|-----------|
| **A** | Digital & Computing | Workstations, Raspberry Pi, Arduino, AI/ML compute |
| **B** | Electronics & Embedded | Soldering stations, ESP32, STM32, oscilloscopes, sensors |
| **C** | Mechanical & Making | 3D printer, power tools, drill press, prototyping materials |
| **D** | Presentation & Ideas | Smart projector, speaker system, whiteboard walls |
| **E** | Infrastructure Learning | Raspberry Pi cluster, NAS, networking node, isolated VLAN |

## Membership Tiers

1. **Observer** — Walk-in access to workshops and demos (open to all CGIT students)
2. **Member** — Supervised equipment use (requires completion of one mini-project)
3. **Core Member** — Extended supervised access (requires peer-reviewed project)
4. **Maintainer** — Zone management and peer training responsibilities

All advancement requires faculty advisor approval. Safety training and code of conduct are mandatory.

## Six Core Goals

1. **Break Department Walls** — Create shared space for cross-discipline collaboration
2. **Make Learning Hands-On** — Complement theory with practical project experience
3. **Build a Culture of Innovation** — Foster ambition from day one
4. **Strengthen CGIT's Reputation** — Hackathons, publications, industry partnerships
5. **Outlast the Founding Batch** — Full documentation and structured handover
6. **Serve the Bastar Region** — Build locally relevant solutions

## Institutional Benefits

- **NAAC Alignment** — Supports Criteria III (Research & Innovation) and V (Student Support)
- **IQAC Compliance** — Documented outcomes for annual reporting
- **AICTE Ecosystem** — Positions CGIT for future funding schemes
- **Outcome-Based Education** — Hands-on projects support OBE program outcomes
- **Employability** — Real portfolios improve placement outcomes
- **Startup Culture** — Nurtures entrepreneurship aligned with national initiatives

## Governance & Safety

- **Faculty Oversight** — Designated Faculty Advisor with sign-off authority
- **Equipment Logging** — All usage recorded in a physical register
- **Safety First** — Mandatory training before any hands-on activity
- **Access Control** — Designated lab hours, entry logging, faculty reachability
- **Code of Conduct** — Signed by all members, enforced by Faculty Advisor
- **Annual Review** — Compliance status submitted to HOD and IQAC

## Funding Strategy

**Zero budget required to launch.** Once operational outcomes are documented, several avenues may be explored:

- **AICTE IDEA Lab Scheme** (~₹1 Cr)
- **AICTE Lab Development Scheme** (~₹30 L)
- **NMDC CSR** — Bastar education focus
- **RUSA 2.0** — Infrastructure grants
- **Smart India Hackathon** — Prize funding
- **e-Yantra & FOSSEE (IIT Bombay)** — Free hardware grants

## Roadmap

- **Week 1–2** — Get permission, identify faculty advisor, locate room
- **Month 1** — Soft launch with existing college equipment
- **Month 2–3** — First cross-department projects, apply for e-Yantra kit
- **Semester 2** — Apply for AICTE funding, register for hackathons
- **Year 2** — Official institutional recognition with budget and constitution

## The Request

Lab Zero asks for **four simple approvals**:

1. **Official Permission** — Recognition as faculty-supervised, student-led body under HOD/Principal oversight
2. **A Room** — One unused or underutilized space (no structural mods needed)
3. **Faculty Advisor** — One faculty member in supervisory role with sign-off authority
4. **IT Approval for VLAN** — Dedicated student network segment (isolated from main campus network)
